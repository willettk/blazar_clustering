E&K+ have argued for emission from distant blazars dominated by cascaded emission from interactions of UHECRs with background photons. Secondaries from UHECR interactions can also significantly contribute to blazar spectra in gamma-rays at lower redshifts, provided that predominant amount of the blazar power is contained in the UHECR and intergalactic magnetic fields (IGMF) are below ~nG (ref).

Recently, studies of VHE-HE spectra of blazars from PKS 1424+240 and 1ES0229+200 have led to claims that secondary emission from UHECRs is responsible for observed hardening at high energy end of the spectra (e.g., PKS 1424+240 (ref)) or even for the entire emission of the source (e.g., PKS 1424+240) (cite E&K PKS1424 paper).

(Sentence about EBL)

Most arguments in favor of secondary emission from UHECR interactions rely on observations of excess flux at higher EBL optical depths, where the primary gamma-ray emission is expected to be drastically attenuated and there is need for an extra emission component. As long as assumptions on the total power in UHECRs are within an acceptable range, these arguments are considered viable. An observable that can rule out UHECR secondaries as significant source of emission in blazars is the detection of short-term variability. Even weak IGMFs will deflect UHECRs enough to smear out any time structures smaller than ~months, making detection of variability on shorter timescales problematic for this scenario.

Moreover, presence of stronger magnetic fields will cause deflections of UHECRs out of the line of sight at shorter timescales than what is required for cascaded emission, effectively eliminating contribution from UHECR secondaries to blazar emission.

In clustered environments, IGMF strengths are expected to be higher (~?) and will damp contribution from UHECR secondaries, whereas underdense environments with IGMFs weaker than ~1nG will allow for cascaded emission. Preferential detection of reduced variability from blazars in less clustered environments could be an indication of significant contribution from UHECR cascaded emission.

(Description of clustering measurement)

We carried out a preliminary investigation of relations between clustering measure and properties of sources in the Third Fermi-LAT Source Catalog (3FGL), including variability (Variablity_Index) for a sample of SDSS blazars with clustering measurement. We excluded low redshift sources (z < 0.15) with highly uncertain clustering measurement and weak 3FGL sources (detection significance < 10 sigma), where the Variability_Index measurement is not sensitive to potential variability because of low statistics. Figure \ref{} shows the 3FGL Variability_Index plotted against the clustering measurement for sources. 3FGL sources with highest Variability_Index appear to lie in more clustered environments.
